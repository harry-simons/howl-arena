8bBest seer & best healer

Small samples (6–22 games/model). Ranked on the most role-controllable skill, not team-dependent win rate. CIs are wide — read as suggestive.

Leads on every component: 0.73 wolf-find (random ≈0.27), converted the find into a lynch in all 6 games, 6/6 village wins. Caveat: only n=6 — suggestively the best, not proven (vil-WR CI floors at 61%).

llama-3.3-70b survives best (58%) and investigates most, but claims the role in 0 of 19 games — its info dies with it.

Worst
nova-lite investigates wolves below chance (0.09) and dies in 88% of its seer games.

Best healer

qwen3-32b

Best other-save rate (0.21) — saving someone else who was attacked, the real read-skill — with balanced, non-hiding play (50% self-protect) and the most other-saves (6).

The misleading stat
qwen3-80b "wins" raw save rate (0.36) but 16 of its 20 saves are self-saves — it self-protects 78% and got targeted while hiding. Genuine other-save rate just 0.07.

Why decompose
Raw save rate rewards cowardice (self-protection + luck). Splitting self vs other saves corrects it.

qwen3-32b tops both power roles' skill metrics despite being mid-table overall — a power-role specialist.

9Cost vs score

Actual input/output prices recovered by least-squares over each model's games (R² ≥ 0.99). Only gemma and mistral-nemo sit on the Pareto frontier — every other model is strictly dominated (something is both cheaper and better).
Pareto-optimaldominated↑ better · ← cheaper
Spend is driven by two independent levers and neither buys rating: price (llama is premium at $0.60/$1.19 per M in/out) and verbosity (the "thinking" models emit 770–980 output tokens/call of reasoning vs mistral's 153). The cheapest-per-token model (gpt-oss-20b) lands mid-cost purely on output volume — for the worst rating. The terse models include both the cheapest and the best.

10Validity

  • 0 void games — every game reached a clean win condition.
  • Positional bias is real but contained. Seat win rates are flat (0.47–0.58), but Day-1 lynches skew to low seats (seat 1: 21% vs seat 6: 4%) because discussion runs in seat order. It does not explain the ladder — flagged to fix in v3 (randomise speaking order).
